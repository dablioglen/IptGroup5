MANILA OCEAN PARK



see on map

Manila Ocean Park is a popular oceanarium and theme park located in Manila, the capital city of the Philippines. It was opened in 2008 and covers an area of 8,000 square meters. The park is home to over 14,000 marine animals from over 277 species. The park features several attractions and exhibits, including the Oceanarium, which is the main attraction of the park. It houses various species of fish, sharks, rays, and other marine creatures. The Oceanarium has a 25-meter underwater tunnel where visitors can walk while viewing the marine life swimming around them. Another popular attraction is the Fish Spa, where visitors can dip their feet into a pool of water and let small fish nibble at the dead skin on their feet, giving them a unique spa experience. The park also has several other attractions such as the Trails to Antarctica, a penguin exhibit, and the Jellies exhibit, which showcases different species of jellyfish. For those who are looking for more thrilling experiences, the park also offers activities such as the Shark and Ray Encounter, where visitors can swim with these magnificent creatures in a controlled environment. Manila Ocean Park is not only a place for entertainment and leisure but also a hub for education and conservation. The park has several programs that aim to educate the public about marine conservation and the importance of preserving the ocean's ecosystems. Overall, Manila Ocean Park is a fun-filled and educational destination that provides visitors with a unique and unforgettable experience.

The Oceanarium: The Oceanarium is the main attraction of Manila Ocean Park, and it's one of the largest aquariums in Asia. It has a 25-meter underwater tunnel that provides a panoramic view of different species of fish, sharks, rays, and other marine animals.

Penguin exhibit: The Trails to Antarctica exhibit is home to Gentoo and Humboldt penguins. Visitors can watch these playful creatures waddle and swim in their habitat.

Jellyfish exhibit: The Jellies exhibit showcases different species of jellyfish in colorful displays. Visitors can also learn about the life cycle and behavior of these fascinating creatures.

Fish spa: The Fish Spa is a unique experience that lets visitors dip their feet into a pool of water and let small fish nibble at the dead skin on their feet, giving them a natural and refreshing foot spa.

Shark and ray encounter: For those seeking adventure, the Shark and Ray Encounter allows visitors to swim with these magnificent creatures in a controlled environment.

Sea Lion Show: The Sea Lion Show is a fun and interactive performance where visitors can watch trained sea lions perform various tricks and stunts.

Conservation programs: Manila Ocean Park is not just a place for entertainment, but also a hub for education and conservation. The park has several programs that aim to educate the public about marine conservation and the importance of preserving the ocean's ecosystems.

Dining options: Manila Ocean Park has several dining options that offer a range of cuisines, from Filipino to international dishes, and even themed restaurants such as the Shark's Den.

Overall, Manila Ocean Park is an amazing destination that offers a unique and unforgettable experience for visitors of all ages.
