Manila, the capital city of the Philippines, has a rich and fascinating history that spans over 400 years. Originally inhabited by the Tagalog people, Manila was already a thriving settlement when the Spanish colonizers arrived in the 16th century. The Spanish established the city as the capital of the Philippines and built a walled city, known as Intramuros, which still stands as a reminder of Manila's colonial past.

Over the years, Manila has undergone many changes, including periods of colonization by the British and the Americans. Today, it is a bustling metropolis with a vibrant mix of cultures and influences, where the modern and traditional coexist. The "Manila Tourist Spot Gallery" website provides a visual journey through the city's history, showcasing some of its most iconic landmarks and attractions. From the historic walls of Intramuros to the modern skyscrapers of Makati, the website offers a glimpse into Manila's past and present, highlighting the diversity and beauty of this dynamic city.
